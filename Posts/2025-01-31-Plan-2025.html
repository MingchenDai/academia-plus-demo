Dreams are worth having—what if they come true?

Plan
2025-02-01

It is the last day of January, and I am on the flight back home from Shenzhen where I spent 9 days. Actually I made some plans in latter weeks of the semester and exam weeks, and have achieved some such as the MCM contest, but there are still many that I haven’t planned or have planned but not achieved yet.

What motivated me to write a plan post is a topic in Shuiyuan titled as Leave a message for yourself one year from now. In my post, I wrote:

My university life should not be limited to average score or GPA. Do you still remember the university life you dreamed several months before the university entrance exam? A life filled with of sports, books and friendships? When was the last time you stepped outside the campus? When was the last time you tried a new dish at a restaurant in the city center, instead of always eating at campus canteens or on Dongchuan Road? When was the last time you researched Shanghai’s public transport network? Have your photography skills improved yet? You might not be able to fully achieve all the goals, but what if you try?

So here it is, the flag post. As the subtitle says:

Dreams are worth having—what if they come true?

This post is just a summary of my goals for 2025. Each goal, along with its progress (or failure), will be documented in a separate diary post.

Language

Improving my English is a challenging but important goal. I scored only 80/100 in the College English 3 course, which contributed to a significant drop in my average score (my rank doubled after the English score was released!). Due to the pale performance in English last semester, I decide to make some changes.

  • CET-6
    I plan to take the exam twice, in June and December of 2025, as CET-6 score is valid for 3 years in Ph.D. application for most universities or academies.
    • [ ] CET-6 in June, 2025
    • [ ] CET-6 in December, 2025
  • TOEFL or ITELS
    This is a long-term goal, as my intention of exchanging aboard is always very vague.
